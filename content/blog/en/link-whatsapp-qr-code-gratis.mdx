## Why a QR code helps more than a link

A link works well online — in a bio, an email signature, a digital ad. But in any physical context (business card, storefront, poster, product packaging), there's nothing to click. That's where the QR code solves it: point the camera, WhatsApp opens, no typing needed.

## What you need before generating your QR code

- **Your phone number**, if you want a link that works today (recommended for most cases).
- **Or your @username** (and Username Key, if you have one), if you'd rather not expose your number — knowing that, for now, whoever scans it needs to search for the name manually.
- An optional pre-filled message, if you want the conversation to start with a specific text (e.g. "Hi, I found you through your poster").

## Step by step in the generator

1. **Choose the mode:** phone number or username, depending on what you'd rather share.
2. **Fill in the details.** In number mode, pick the country (the code fills in automatically) and type the number without the leading zero. In username mode, just type the name (and the optional Key).
3. **Add a pre-filled message**, if you want — it's optional, but useful for business contexts ("I'd like to know more about X").
4. **Confirm the generated link.** The site immediately shows you the final URL and the matching QR code, updated in real time as you type.
5. **Download the QR code** in whichever format you need:
   - **PNG** — the most universal, good for social media and digital documents.
   - **JPEG** — alternative to PNG, lighter file, no transparency.
   - **SVG** — vector-based, ideal if you're printing at large scale (poster, storefront) without losing quality.
   - **PDF** — ready to print directly, useful for taking to a print shop.
6. **Also copy the text link**, with the "Copy all" button, in case you want to share it in writing in addition to the QR code.

## Where to use your QR code

- **Business card** — saves space and avoids number transcription errors.
- **Storefront or store counter** — customers reach you without you needing to be available at that moment.
- **Email signature** — alternative to writing out the number in full.
- **Product packaging or invoice** — good for requesting after-sales support directly.
- **Social media** (as an image) — when the platform doesn't allow direct clickable links (e.g. Instagram bio as a fixed image).

## Best practices when printing a QR code

- Always test the printed QR code before producing it in bulk — very small sizes or glossy paper can make it hard to scan.
- Make sure there's enough contrast between the code and the background — avoid printing over colored backgrounds or images.
- Leave a blank margin around the code (the "quiet zone") — without it, some cameras have trouble recognizing the pattern.

## Summary

Generating a free WhatsApp QR code takes less than a minute: choose number or username, fill in the details, and download in whichever format you need — PNG, JPEG, SVG or PDF. For printed physical contexts, the QR code saves friction that a link alone can't solve.
